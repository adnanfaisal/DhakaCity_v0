Dhaka, the capital city of Bangladesh, is a vibrant and bustling metropolis with a rich cultural heritage. Located on the banks of the Buriganga River, Dhaka has a history dating back to the 7th century. The city boasts a blend of Mughal, Islamic, and colonial architectural influences. Dhaka is known for its vibrant markets, including New Market and Gulshan Market, offering a wide range of local handicrafts, textiles, and street food. The city is home to numerous historical landmarks, such as the Lalbagh Fort, Ahsan Manzil, and the Liberation War Museum. Dhaka's culinary scene is famous for its traditional Bangladeshi cuisine, including dishes like biryani, kebabs, and mishti doi (sweet yogurt). With a population of over 20 million, Dhaka is one of the most densely populated cities in the world. The city faces challenges like traffic congestion, pollution, and poverty but remains a hub for education, commerce, and culture. Dhaka is also known for its festive atmosphere, particularly during Eid-al-Fitr, Eid-al-Adha, and the Bengali New Year (Pohela Boishakh). Despite its challenges, Dhaka's warm hospitality, resilience, and vibrant energy make it a unique and captivating city.
